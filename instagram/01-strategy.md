# Trowel and Code — Instagram Brand Awareness Strategy

> Goal for this campaign: get trades businesses and suppliers to **recognise the name "Trowel and Code"** and associate it with one idea — *"they get you to the top of Google and the leads come free."*
> This is an awareness play first, lead-gen second. We are buying recognition now so the enquiries compound later.

---

## 1. The strategy in one line

**Make tradespeople feel the pain of being invisible on Google, prove we fix it, and never sound like the lead-gen companies that burned them.**

Everything below serves that line.

---

## 2. Who we're actually talking to

(From market research — UK/Ireland trades owners: plumbers, builders, electricians, roofers, landscapers, HVAC, and trade suppliers.)

- **They're on tools 8–5.** They scroll at lunch and after tea (6–9pm), Tue–Thu strongest. Weekends are dead.
- **85% admit they don't know how to use social media well** (plumbers worst at 90%). They know they *should* show up online but feel lost — that's our opening, not our insult.
- **Their strongest emotion is lead-gen rage.** Real sentiment from forums: one lead sold to 4 trades at ~£25 each; "race to the bottom on price"; locked-in contracts where "leads dry up once you're tied in"; ~£600/yr Checkatrade subs they resent. **We are the anti-Checkatrade.**
- **They've been burned by websites too:** "I paid for a site and it doesn't even come up when you search for me."
- **They live and die by Google.** 80%+ of homeowners search online before hiring; the Map Pack top-3 takes ~44% of local clicks and most of the calls. Your clients already feel this — it's why they "only care about Google Business." We lean *into* that, we don't fight it.
- **They want plain English.** No jargon, no acronyms, no agency fluff. Straight-talking reads as *more* expert to this crowd, not less.

---

## 3. Positioning & brand voice

**Positioning:** Trowel and Code is the tech lead trades businesses wish they had on staff. We make Google send you free leads forever instead of renting them from a lead-gen site.

**The name is the hook.** *Trowel* = the trade. *Code* = the tech. We're the only ones who speak both. Use this everywhere: "We speak Trowel and Code."

**Voice rules:**
- Talk like a foreman, not a marketer. Short sentences. No "leverage," "synergy," "solutions."
- Confident and a bit blunt. "Stop paying for leads you'll never win."
- Proof over polish — ranking screenshots, real call volumes, before/after sites beat slick graphics.
- Never use a stat without making it sting. Not "44% of clicks go to the top 3" → "If you're not top 3, half your customers never even see you."

**The one message we repeat until it's annoying:**
> *"We don't sell you leads. We make Google send you free ones — and you own that, forever."*

## 4. Content pillars (the mix you asked for)

Three pillars in rotation. Roughly **40 / 35 / 25**.

### Pillar A — Attention / Brand (40%)
The "stop the scroll" content that gets reach from people who've never heard of you. This is where your **"guess the brand"** idea lives (see `03-reel-scripts.md`). Goal: reach + shares + follows. Not selling.

### Pillar B — Pain & Proof (35%)
The content that converts the attention into "...wait, this is me." Anti-lead-gen rants, before/after Google rankings, client results with real numbers, myth-busting. This pillar carries the message. Goal: saves + DMs.

### Pillar C — What We Actually Do (25%)
The straight posts about your service — how GBP optimisation works, what a top-3 ranking means in £, the process of working with you, FAQs. Goal: turn warm followers into enquiries. Keep it useful, not brochure-y.

> Why this split: research is clear that awareness content (A) drives reach but doesn't convert, and service content (C) converts but won't grow you. Pillar B is the bridge — it's where most of your best content should sit, and it's the cheapest to make (you, talking, with proof on screen).

## 5. The flagship campaign — "Household Name"

Your "guess the brand" reel becomes a **recurring series**, not a one-off. Research is blunt on two points:

1. **The format works but is saturated.** "Guess the logo" reels are everywhere. We differentiate by (a) making it a *named weekly series* people follow for, and (b) tying every episode back to *one* recognition lesson for trades.
2. **Showing real logos (McDonald's etc.) in your own promo is a genuine legal risk.** Nominative fair use lets you *name* a brand, but using the stylised logo to sell *your* service weakens that defence and raises implied-endorsement/dilution risk — and small businesses are more exposed, not less. **Fix: use obscured / silhouette / colour-block versions** (which is also a *better* quiz — guessing a half-hidden logo is more fun than a full one).

Full script + the follow-up "so why doesn't anyone know yours?" payoff is in `03-reel-scripts.md`. The series name and recurring hook live there too.

## 6. Cadence & format mix

- **3–5 feed posts per week + daily Stories.** Consistency beats volume — gaps kill reach.
- **~60% Reels, ~25% carousels, ~15% static.** Reels do discovery; carousels do saves/depth; static for announcements.
- **Best windows:** Tue–Thu, post by ~11:30am (catch lunch) or ~5:30pm (catch after-tea). Avoid weekends for feed posts; use them for Stories only.
- Don't cross-post the identical asset everywhere — the algorithm now down-ranks obvious recycling.

See `02-content-calendar.md` for a ready-to-run 30-day calendar.

## 7. How the algorithm pays you back (2026)

Make content for these signals, in order:
1. **Sends-per-reach (DM shares) — the #1 signal.** Every reel should be *taggable*: "tag the mate whose website is invisible."
2. **Completion rate** — keep reels 15–30s, hook in the first 2 seconds, on-screen text *before* any voiceover.
3. **Saves** — carousels and checklists ("save this before you renew Checkatrade").
4. **Comments** — ask one easy question.
5. **Likes barely count.** Stop optimising for them.

Captions are SEO: front-load your keyword in the first 125 characters ("Google Business Profile for tradesmen…"). Use **3–5 hashtags only** (1 broad + 2–3 niche + 1 branded like #TrowelAndCode), not 30.

## 8. Turning attention into enquiries (without being salesy)

Your audience hates being sold to and you refuse to pay for leads — so the funnel is built on *value first*.

- **DM keyword funnel beats "link in bio."** Keyword-trigger CTAs convert at ~5–15% vs ~1–3% for bio links. Use: *"Comment RANK and I'll send you the free checklist."* → auto/manual DM the lead magnet → soft-qualify → offer a free 15-min ranking review.
- **Lead magnet:** a one-page *"Free Google Business Profile Audit"* or *"7 things knocking you out of Google's top 3."* This is the soft entry point — gives value, costs you nothing, and self-selects people who care about Google (which is your whole audience).
- **Bio = a CTA, not a tagline.** Example:
  > Trowel and Code 🧱💻
  > We get trades top-3 on Google — so the leads come free.
  > No lead-gen sites. No locked contracts.
  > 👇 Free ranking checklist
- **Bio link + DMs + content all point at the same one action.** Don't scatter.
- **Stories** carry the soft asks: polls ("Are you paying for leads? 😬 yes / no"), Q&A, screenshots of client wins.

---

## 9. Metrics that matter (and the ones to ignore)

**Track monthly:**
- Reach from non-followers (is the awareness play working?)
- Shares + saves per post (the real currency)
- Follower growth rate
- DM conversations started / checklist requests
- Free reviews booked → clients won (the only number that pays the bills)

**Ignore:** likes, vanity follower count, "impressions." They don't correlate with enquiries in 2026.

---

## 10. Reality check & timeline

Be honest with yourself on this:

- **Organic reach is declining** (~6.8% for accounts under 100K, down from ~12% in 2023). Brand recognition is a *months* game, not weeks. Expect the first 60–90 days to look quiet on enquiries while reach and follows build.
- **Consistency is the whole strategy.** 3 posts a week for 6 months beats 15 posts in week one and nothing after.
- **When you have 3–4 reels that over-performed organically, put a small paid boost behind them** to accelerate recognition. You're not buying leads — you're amplifying content that already proved it works. That stays true to your no-paid-leads principle.
- **Repurpose relentlessly:** every reel → a carousel → 3 Stories → a still. One idea, five posts.
