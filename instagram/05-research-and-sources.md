## A. UK tradesmen pain points (your content fuel)

### Late payment / cash flow — the most defensible, most emotive stat
- **81% of UK tradespeople are chasing late payments, owed ~£6,210 on average.** 73% chasing multiple overdue invoices; 8% have written off >£10,000. ([Direct Line via Total Contractor](https://www.total-contractor.co.uk/payment-pains-survey-shows-81-of-tradespeople-are-chasing-late-payments/), [NationalWorld](https://www.nationalworld.com/business/pay-up-the-average-tradesperson-is-chasing-ps6000-in-late-payments-4814488))
- Construction is the **worst-hit UK sector for late payment (95%)**. ([Coface 2025](https://www.coface.com/news-economy-and-insights/2025-uk-payment-survey-companies-face-rising-payment-delays-amid-buyer-cash-flow-concerns))

### Costs, skills, burnout
- **79%** say rising material/tool costs are stopping their business growing; **71%** say skills shortages stop them expanding. ([Checkatrade Trade Nation report](https://www.checkatrade.com/blog/news/trade-nation-report/))
- **9 in 10 felt stressed in 2025; 82% reported burnout.** ([Logic4training](https://www.logic4training.co.uk/insights/challenges-facing-uk-trades-businesses-in-2025/), [IronmongeryDirect](https://www.ironmongerydirect.co.uk/research/mental-health-in-the-trades))

### Lead-gen sites — the grievance your whole brand is built against
- **Checkatrade ~£70–£120+VAT/month on a 12-month auto-renewing contract, PLUS per-lead fees (~£5–£40).** MyBuilder pay-per-bid ~£2–£20. Bark credits ~£5–£40/lead, expiring in 3 months. ([SwiftLead](https://www.swiftlead.co.uk/blog/checkatrade-cost-for-tradesmen), [LocalSearchNorth](https://localsearchnorth.uk/blog/checkatrade-vs-bark-vs-mybuilder/))
- **One lead is sold to 3–4 different trades.** "You could pay £30 for a lead that also went to your competitors — and only one of you gets the job." ([SwiftLead](https://www.swiftlead.co.uk/blog/checkatrade-vs-mybuilder-vs-bark))
- Bark reviewers report paying for "fake or completely unresponsive" leads, refused refunds. ([Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/bark.com))
- **The killer line for your brand:** *"The best tradesmen don't use these lead-gen sites — they are never short of work."* ([MoneySavingExpert](https://forums.moneysavingexpert.com/discussion/5787628/warning-rated-people-and-mybuilder))

### Website / SEO frustration (validates your offer directly)
- Tradesmen report spending "thousands without ever seeing a proper report," being sold "guaranteed rankings" for worthless keywords, then the provider disappears. ([Search Engine Journal](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/top-seo-scams/416351/), [DotNexus](https://dotnexus.net/7-common-seo-mistakes-tradesmen-make-and-how-to-avoid-them/))
- A proper site + Google reviews "could replace platform fees entirely." ([Trusted Tradesman](https://www.trustedtradesman.co.uk/tradesmen-get-found-on-google-without-a-website/))

### How customers actually find trades
- ~**69% word-of-mouth**, ~**75% use some online method**, 33% ask their social network. ([Eureka Research](https://www.eurekaresearch.co.uk/the-future-of-finding-tradespeople/), [Fix Radio](https://www.fixradio.co.uk/fix-feed/construction-news/post/nearly-50-of-consumers-dont-trust-the-tradespeople-they-employ/))

> ⚠️ **Caveats:** MoneySavingExpert/review pages blocked automated access, so a few quotes are paraphrases — verify exact wording before publishing. Checkatrade/insurer "optimism" stats have a vested interest; lean on the late-payment and lead-gen-cost numbers, which are the most defensible. The "% of UK trades with a website" figure is stale (2020, ~45% on social) — don't cite a hard number; say "many."

## B. What statistically grows accounts like yours

### The honest headline
**Most "agency grew +150% in 6 months" case studies online are unverifiable marketing fiction** — no named business, no methodology. The trustworthy data comes from large aggregate datasets. Build on these, not on blog brag-numbers.

### Measured levers (trust these)
- **Replying to every comment is the single strongest engagement signal** across platforms (Buffer, 52M-post dataset). Cheapest lever you have. ([Buffer 2026](https://buffer.com/resources/state-of-social-media-engagement-2026/))
- **Carousels win engagement + saves (~109% more engagement per reach than Reels); Reels win reach (~36% more).** Use Reels to get found, carousels to be saved. ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/state-of-social-media-engagement-2026/), [Socialinsider](https://www.socialinsider.io/social-media-benchmarks/instagram))
- **Short wins:** 7–15s reels ~74% completion vs ~49% for 30–60s. Retention beats length. ([OpusClip](https://www.opus.pro/blog/ideal-instagram-reels-length))
- **DM sends** are the strongest signal to *new* audiences — make content taggable/shareable. ([Metricool](https://metricool.com/instagram-reel-analytics/))
- **Low-production beats polished:** a UGC-style iPhone video (team as "users") drove **217% more leads at 45% lower cost-per-lead** than the influencer version. ([Superside via Stack Influence](https://stackinfluence.com/virality-to-roi-tiktok-short-form-video-trends/))

### Benchmarks (directional — vendor-sourced)
- 3–5 posts/week ≈ >2x follower growth vs 1–2/week (direction solid, exact multiplier is a vendor estimate). ([HopperHQ](https://www.hopperhq.com/blog/instagram-posting-frequency-2025/))
- B2B engagement is structurally lower (0.5–2% vs 3–10% B2C) — set expectations. ([Triple Dart](https://www.tripledart.com/b2b-marketing/instagram-for-b2b-marketing))

### Realistic timeline
- Organic B2B: **~3–6 months to meaningful traction, 6–12 months to pipeline impact.** Algorithms need ~50–100 posts to categorise an account. ([Funnl](https://funnl.ai/how-long-does-social-media-marketing-take-results/))
- **The only firmly-measured ROI was from PAID short-form** (Northbeam: 15 advertisers, ~$7M spend → ~$10M attributed, ~3x lift). Translation: prove a reel organically, then boost the winners. ([Northbeam](https://www.northbeam.io/post/2025-marketing-trends-to-watch-out-for-part-2))

## C. What real accounts in your space actually post (teardown)

### Agencies serving trades
- **Hook Agency (@hookagency, ~15K)** — talking-head Reels all hooked on "CONTRACTORS…", before/afters with location tags, whiteboard explainers. Runs a **separate meme page @roofermemes** for reach. ([profile](https://www.instagram.com/hookagency/), [post ideas](https://hookagency.com/blog/roofing-social-media-post-ideas/))
- **Relentless Digital (@relentless_digital)** — founder-fronted "stop making this marketing mistake" Reels + results bragging.

### "Marketing for tradesmen" creators (UK)
- **Trade Mastermind (@trademastermind, ~21K) / Lewis Raymond Taylor (~205K)** — almost entirely **client testimonial Reels with a £ revenue figure as the hook** ("In November I installed 12 boilers worth £50,000"). Proof + numbers is the whole model.
- **On The Tools (@onthetools)** — UK's biggest trades community; entertainment-first, user-submitted funny on-site clips, memes, brand partnerships. The audience-aggregator to study (and potentially partner with).

### Tradespeople who went viral (your reach playbook)
- **The Pool Guy (~14.8M TikTok)** — "green-to-clean" transformations, timelapse, a **"difficulty rating" hook** ("7/10 difficulty"), signature catchphrase. Single clips 1.4M+ likes.
- **The Vibing Plasterer (~2.1M)** — personality + craft; grooving on site over satisfying smooth-finish clips.
- **R Davis Electrical (~490K)** — educational listicles for apprentices ("10 tools every apprentice needs").
- **Pattern across all:** transformation / timelapse / satisfying process + a persona or catchphrase. **This is the model for your filmed footage (resin pours etc.).**

### "Guess the brand" format in the wild
- Execution: blurred/partial logo → beat/countdown → reveal, with **"comment your score"** driving the engagement loop. Rapid-fire 5–15s. ([example](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DQacZ3EDIcK/))
- **Your differentiation (this is the upgrade):** do **"guess the TOOL/TRADE brand"** — DeWalt, Makita, Milwaukee, Festool, Screwfix, Wickes, Snickers. Native to your exact audience, more shareable inside the trade, and far less exposed than using McDonald's to sell your service. See `03-reel-scripts.md`.
