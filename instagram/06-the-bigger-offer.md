# The Bigger Offer — Google Business as the Front Door

You said it best: **Google Business is the entry point to each customer.** It's the foot in the door — the easy "yes" that wins trust. The bigger opportunity is everything *behind* that door, because trades are one of the least-digitised industries on earth and they know it.

This doc is both a **business-strategy expansion** and a **content goldmine** — every gap below is a pain you can post about.

---

## 1. The macro story (your credibility hook)

- **Construction is the 2nd least-digitised industry in the world** — only agriculture ranks lower (McKinsey Global Institute digitisation index). It scored ~1.4 vs a cross-sector average of ~3.0. ([McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations/our-insights/decoding-digital-transformation-in-construction))
- **~870,000 UK construction SMEs (99.9% of construction firms)** — a huge, fragmented, under-served base. ([BrieXO](https://briexo.com/blog/uk-construction-sme-digital-transformation))
- The tech that serves them is growing fast: **Field Service Management software ~12–13% CAGR to ~$11.8B by 2030**; **PropTech ~16% CAGR, roughly doubling to ~$100B by 2030**; online home-services booking ~14–17% CAGR. ([Grand View – FSM](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/field-service-management-market), [PropTech](https://www.openpr.com/news/4304152/proptech-market-is-growing-at-a-cagr-of-16-3-during-the-2025-2035))

> ⚠️ Reality check on your "30%+ by 2030": the *market roughly doubling* by 2030 is right, but the credible annual growth rate (CAGR) is **mid-teens, not 30%**. Use "the market's set to double by 2030" — it's accurate and still punchy. All market-size figures are research-firm estimates; present as ranges.

**The one-liner:** *"The trades are the last big industry the tech wave hasn't reached. We're the ones bringing it to them — starting with Google."*

## 2. The reframe: a ladder, not a single service

Google Business gets them found. But a found business with a leaky back office still loses money. Position Trowel and Code as the tech lead who fixes the whole journey:

```
GET FOUND          →   GET THE JOB        →   GET PAID           →   GET REBOOKED
(entry point)          (convert)              (the painful bit)      (compound)
Google Business        Website that ranks     Automated invoicing    Automated review
Profile + local SEO    + online booking       + payment chasing      requests → more
                       + fast quoting                                 Google reviews →
                                                                      ranks higher → loop
```

The beauty: **it's a loop.** More Google reviews → higher ranking → more leads. Getting them paid faster and gathering reviews automatically *feeds the very thing you sold them first.* That's the story.

## 3. What they're actually struggling with (beyond Google), ranked by pain

1. **Getting paid.** The strongest data of all: **81% of tradespeople are chasing late payments, owed ~£6,210 on average**; construction is the worst-hit UK sector (95%). ([Direct Line](https://www.directlinegroup.co.uk/en/news/brand-news/2024/081020242.html)) → *Automated invoicing + payment chasing is the highest-ROI fix you can offer.*
2. **Quoting & admin.** Quotes in Word, customer details lost in WhatsApp/texts; trades lose a reported **~7 hrs/week (~48 days/year) to paperwork**. ([PHAM News](https://phamnews.co.uk/the-hidden-cost-of-being-over-burdened-by-admin/) — vendor-sourced, treat as directional)
3. **Reviews & reputation.** **68% of UK consumers use reviews as their primary source** when choosing; recency matters. Automated review requests after every job feed the Google loop. ([Reputation.com](https://reputation.com/press-room/uk-consumers-rely-on-reviews-more-than-ever-despite-ai-concerns/))
4. **Scheduling / job management.** Run "across WhatsApp threads and scraps of paper."
5. **Customer comms, online booking, lead follow-up.** Missed calls and slow replies = lost jobs. Booking is the fastest-growing home-services tech segment — unmet demand.

## 4. What Trowel and Code can change (the offer)

You don't need to build software — the tools exist (Tradify, Jobber, Commusoft, Payaca, Powered Now, ServiceTitan). **The gap is that trades can't or won't set them up.** Top adoption blockers are human, not technical: skills/integration (~44%), "don't understand how to use it" (38%), unclear ROI (37%); **42% of construction firms say their workforce isn't digitally ready.** ([BrieXO](https://briexo.com/blog/uk-construction-sme-digital-transformation), [ServiceTitan](https://www.servicetitan.com/press/ai-in-the-skilled-trades-report-2025))

**So your product is implementation + outcomes, not software.** Trades buy "I get paid on time and don't touch a spreadsheet," not "a SaaS subscription." Concrete services to ladder into:

- **The glue layer** — connect GBP → website enquiry → quote → job → invoice → automated payment chase → automated review request. No single tool does this end-to-end for a one-van business; you orchestrate it. *This is the gap you uniquely own.*
- **Done-for-you automated payment chasing / embedded payments** — directly attacks the £6,210 wound.
- **Automated review generation** tied back to Google — feeds the ranking you sold them.
- **AI/comms automation** — auto-reply to missed calls and DMs, follow up dead quotes, nurture leads. ~Half of trades are already curious about AI but don't know where to start.
- **Online booking + fast mobile quoting** setup.

**Appetite is there:** 88% of UK tradespeople say digital tools are important and 89% already use *something* — the bottleneck is implementation, which is exactly your product. ([Electrical Contracting News](https://electricalcontractingnews.com/news/digital-tools-key-to-navigating-challenges-for-uk-tradespeople/))

## 5. How this powers the Instagram campaign

Each gap above is content. The bigger offer doesn't replace the Google message — it *deepens* the page so you're not a one-note account:

- **Pain reels:** "Still chasing invoices by text?" / "7 hours a week on paperwork — here's the fix."
- **The loop carousel:** "Get found → win the job → get paid → get the review → rank higher. Round and round." (great saver)
- **Authority angle:** "Construction is the 2nd least-digitised industry on earth. We're changing that for [town]'s trades."
- **Funnel ladder:** Google Business checklist is still the lead magnet (the easy entry). Once they're in the DMs, *that's* where the bigger conversation about automation happens.

> Keep the front-of-house message simple — **"we get you top 3 on Google, free leads"** — because that's the easy yes. Reveal the bigger offer once you've earned attention. Lead with the door, not the whole house.

## 6. Caveats (be honest in your own materials)
- The £6,210 late-payment figure is a small sample (n=145, Direct Line/Opinium 2024) — defensible but don't over-claim.
- Admin-hours, AI-adoption and "engagement" stats are largely vendor-sponsored (Powered Now, ServiceTitan, Autodesk) — directional, not gospel.
- Market-size/CAGR numbers vary by research firm — present as ranges and as "set to roughly double by 2030," not a single hard %.
